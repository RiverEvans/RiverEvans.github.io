River Evans

I’m a Grade 12 student interested in robotics and biomedical systems, with a focus on wearable technology. I enjoy building systems that combine mechanical design, electronics, and software into one working product. My problem-solving approach depends on the project. Sometimes I design and model first. Other times I prototype early and refine through testing. I value iteration and real-world performance. I’ve worked with Autodesk, Arduino, and 3D printing to develop embedded and mechanical systems. One project I’m especially proud of is a radar tracking arm that integrates ultrasonic sensing with motion control. I’m interested in human-centered design, biomechanics, automation, and optimization, and I aim to build systems that are efficient, accessible, and practical. I plan to study mechatronics engineering and pursue a master’s degree. I’m always looking for ways to improve a design, even after it works, and I care about understanding why systems behave the way they do.
